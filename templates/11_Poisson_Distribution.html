Example

The chance of an earthquake occurring in Bangalore is one in 100 years. What is the probability of an earthquake occurring in Bangalore today? This can be described using the Poisson distribution.

Poisson Distribution

  • Poisson distribution arises when the number of occurrences is large, but the probability of each occurrence is small.
  • Parameter λ represents the average rate of occurrence.
  • Probability mass function of Poisson distribution: P(X = x) = (e^(-λ) * λ^x) / x! for x = 0, 1, 2, ...

Limiting Case of Binomial Distribution

Poisson distribution can be considered a limiting case of binomial distribution when the number of trials approaches infinity and the probability of success approaches zero, while the product of the two remains constant.

Transformation to Standard Normal Distribution

By defining Z = (X - E[X]) / √Var(X), the Poisson distribution can be transformed into a standard normal variable Z, where E[X] = λ and Var(X) = λ.
