Normal Distribution Tutorial

Welcome to our video on normal distribution! In this tutorial, you will learn about the definition, characteristics, transformation to standard normal distribution, and application of normal distribution in statistical inference.

1. Definition of Normal Distribution

A continuous random variable \( x \) is said to have a normal distribution with mean \( \mu \) and variance \( \sigma^2 \). Its probability density function is given by:

\( f(x) = \frac{1}{\sigma \sqrt{2\pi}} e^{-\frac{(x-\mu)^2}{2\sigma^2}} \)

The normal distribution is characterized by a bell-shaped curve, which is symmetrical around the mean \( \mu \).

2. Characteristics of Normal Distribution

  • The mean, median, and mode of a normal distribution are all equal.
  • The point of inflection is at \( \pm \) one standard deviation from the mean.
  • The measure of skewness (mean - mode) / standard deviation is always zero.

3. Area Under the Curve

Approximately 68% of the observations fall within one standard deviation from the mean, 95% within two standard deviations, and 99.9% within three standard deviations.

4. Transformation to Standard Normal Distribution

A normal distribution can be transformed into a standard normal distribution using the formula \( z = \frac{x - \mu}{\sigma} \), where \( x \) is the original score, \( \mu \) is the mean, and \( \sigma \) is the standard deviation.

5. Application in Statistical Inference

Normal distribution is widely used in statistical inference. For example, in testing differences between means of two populations, z-test can be applied using standard normal distribution.

Example: Analysis of Test Scores

A teacher wants to analyze test scores of students using a normal distribution. Scores follow a bell curve, and the teacher can use the standard normal distribution to find probabilities of different score ranges.

Verbal GRE scores of boys and girls follow normal distributions with known variances. A z-test can be conducted to determine if there is a significant difference in mean scores between boys and girls.

In conclusion, normal distribution plays a crucial role in statistical analysis, and understanding its properties and applications is essential for various fields.
