Uniform Distribution Tutorial

Welcome to our video on uniform distribution! In this tutorial, you will learn about the definition, example, and application of uniform distribution.

1. Definition of Uniform Distribution

A continuous random variable \( x \) is said to have a uniform distribution if its probability density function in the interval \( [a, b] \) remains constant, given by:

\[ f(x) = \begin{cases} \frac{1}{b - a}, & \text{if } a \leq x \leq b \\ 0, & \text{otherwise} \end{cases} \]

In this distribution, the probability remains the same for all values taken by \( x \) between \( a \) and \( b \).

2. Example of Uniform Distribution

In a research department of a steel manufacturer, three different types of steel sheets have thicknesses ranging from 150 to 200 millimeters. A randomly selected sheet of steel will have a probability of \( \frac{1}{50} \) for any value in the interval 150 to 200 millimeters.

3. Application of Uniform Distribution

The uniform distribution is useful in generating random variables and simulating systems where probabilities remain constant within a specified interval. For example, in a queuing system, the arrival of customers can be modeled using a uniform distribution.
